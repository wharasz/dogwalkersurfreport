What does point it to vercel mean

Apr 17
Prepared straightforward explanation for technical terminology

Great question! Here's the plain-English version:

When someone types dogwalkersurfreport.com into a browser, the internet needs to know which computer to fetch the website from. That's what "pointing" a domain does — it's just telling the domain registrar (like Namecheap) the address of the server where your site lives.

The steps in plain English:

  1. You buy the domain from Namecheap — they're now in charge of where that name points
  2. You put your website's code on Vercel (you just drag and drop a folder, or connect a GitHub repo)
  3. In Namecheap's settings, you change a setting called the nameservers to Vercel's address — basically saying "hey, when anyone asks where dogwalkersurfreport.com lives, ask Vercel"
  4. Within a few hours, the whole internet knows to go to Vercel when someone visits your site

It's like forwarding your mail to a new address. You tell the post office (Namecheap) that all letters for your name should go to your new house (Vercel).

Vercel walks you through this with a step-by-step UI when you add a custom domain to your project — you don't need to understand DNS deeply, they basically just tell you exactly what to copy and paste where.

Here's the exact step-by-step — you'll be going back and forth between Squarespace and Vercel a couple times:

Step 1 — In Vercel (do this first)

  1. Create a free account at vercel.com
  2. Create a new project (even a blank one for now)
  3. Go to your project → Settings → Domains
  4. Type in dogwalkersurfreport.com and hit Add
  5. Vercel will show you two values — an A record (looks like an IP address, e.g. 76.76.21.21) and a CNAME value (looks like cname.vercel-dns.com). Leave this tab open.

Step 2 — In Squarespace

  1. Go to your Squarespace account → Domains
  2. Click on dogwalkersurfreport.com
  3. Find DNS Settings or Custom Records
  4. You need to add two records:
    • A record → Host: @ → Value: the IP address Vercel gave you
    • CNAME record → Host: www → Value: cname.vercel-dns.com
  5. Save both

Step 3 — Wait It can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours for the internet to catch up, though usually it's under an hour. You'll know it worked when Vercel shows a green checkmark next to your domain.

That's genuinely it. The trickiest part is just finding where Squarespace hides the DNS settings — it's usually under the domain's settings, labeled "Custom Records" or "DNS Settings."
